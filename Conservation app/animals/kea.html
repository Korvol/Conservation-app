Kea

All About the Kea

The Kea (scientific name: *Nestor notabilis*) is a fascinating and highly intelligent parrot endemic to the South Island of New Zealand. Renowned for its curiosity and playful nature, it's the only alpine parrot in the world, thriving in the harsh environments of the Southern Alps.

Key Facts & Characteristics

Endemic to New Zealand

The Kea is found exclusively in the Southern Alps of the South Island, making it a unique treasure of Aotearoa's natural heritage.

Only Alpine Parrot

Unlike most parrots that prefer warmer climates, Kea are uniquely adapted to live year-round in the cold, mountainous regions, often seen playing in the snow.

Highly Intelligent

Known for their problem-solving abilities and curiosity, Kea often investigate new objects by pecking and manipulating them, sometimes to the amusement (or frustration) of tourists.

Distinctive Appearance

Kea have olive-green plumage with brilliant orange feathers under their wings, visible when they fly. They have strong, curved beaks suited for their varied diet.

Social & Playful

They are highly social birds, often seen in small groups. Their playful and mischievous nature can lead them to explore cars, backpacks, and anything left unattended, earning them the nickname "mountain clown."

Conservation Status & Population

With no official precise count, professionals estimate that **between 1,000 and 5,000 Kea** are left in the wild. This makes them a critically endangered species, listed as **Nationally Endangered** within New Zealand.

Their dwindling numbers highlight the urgent need for conservation efforts to protect this unique bird.

Threats to the Kea

Kea face multiple threats that contribute to their endangered status:

  • Predation: Introduced predators such as stoats, cats, and possums prey on Kea eggs, chicks, and even adult birds, especially during nesting season.
  • Human Impacts: Direct human persecution (historically due to perceived livestock threats), lead poisoning from old buildings, and accidental ingestion of harmful substances are significant dangers.
  • Urban Accidents: As Kea venture closer to human settlements and tourist areas, they face risks like being hit by cars, getting entangled in traps, or electrocution from power lines.
  • Habitat Loss/Degradation: While they are adaptable, changes to their natural alpine habitat due to climate change and human activity can also impact their food sources and breeding grounds.

Where to See Them

Kea are typically found in the alpine and forested regions of the South Island. Popular spots where you might encounter them include:

  • Arthur's Pass National Park: One of the most well-known places to see Kea.
  • Fiordland National Park: Including areas around Milford Sound and Homer Tunnel.
  • Mount Aspiring National Park
  • Otago region: High country areas.

While encountering Kea can be exciting, it's important to observe them from a distance and avoid feeding them, as this can lead to dependency and expose them to further risks.

Conservation Efforts

Various organizations, including the Department of Conservation (DOC) and the Kea Conservation Trust, are working to protect Kea through:

  • Predator control programs.
  • Research and monitoring of Kea populations.
  • Public education campaigns to reduce harmful human interactions.
  • Lead removal initiatives in their habitats.

Every effort helps in ensuring the survival of this remarkable and intelligent parrot for future generations.
