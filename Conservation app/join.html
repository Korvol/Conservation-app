Join Our Conservation Efforts

Get Involved and Be a Kaitiaki!

There are many ways you can contribute to protecting Aotearoa's unique natural heritage. Whether you have a lot of time or just a little, every effort helps us safeguard our wild heart for future generations.

Volunteer

Join a local conservation group for practical work like pest trapping, planting native trees, or weeding. Check the Department of Conservation (DOC) website for opportunities near you, or inquire at local visitor centers.

Report Sightings or Issues

If you spot an injured animal, illegal activity, or a new pest plant/animal, please report it. Use our Report an Issue page to send us details, or contact DOC directly.

Donate

Financial contributions help fund vital conservation projects, from predator control and species recovery programs to research and education. Consider donating to a reputable conservation organization.

Educate Yourself and Others

Learn more about New Zealand's unique biodiversity and the threats it faces. Share your knowledge with friends and family, and encourage responsible behavior in nature.

Be a Responsible Visitor

When exploring New Zealand's natural areas, remember to 'Leave No Trace.' Stick to marked trails, take all your rubbish with you, and never feed wild animals. Clean your gear to prevent the spread of kauri dieback and other diseases.

Together, as kaitiaki (guardians), we can make a difference!
